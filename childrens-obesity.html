What is Childhood Obesity?

Childhood obesity is a medical condition where excess body fat negatively impacts a child's health or well-being. In the UK, it is typically assessed using the Body Mass Index (BMI) adjusted for a child's age and sex. Children with a BMI at or above the 95th percentile are classified as obese.

Health Risks

Obese children are at a higher risk of developing several chronic conditions, including:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Sleep apnoea
  • Joint problems
  • Low self-esteem and depression

Causes

Childhood obesity is caused by a combination of factors, such as:

  • Poor diet (high in sugar, fat, and processed foods)
  • Physical inactivity (excessive screen time, lack of exercise)
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Environmental and socio-economic factors

Prevention and Management

Preventing and managing childhood obesity involves a holistic approach:

  • Encouraging a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
  • Promoting at least 60 minutes of physical activity daily
  • Limiting sugary drinks and snacks
  • Creating supportive environments at home, in schools, and in communities
  • Involving healthcare professionals when needed
